Restaurant Website

A mobile first redesign for a restaurant website

Tools used in this project

Requirements Workshops

Analytics

Usability Testing

Context

An opportunity to redesign a restaurant website. It begins with a requirements workshop held with clients to develop insight into user and business requirements. Google Analytics key metrics were analyzed to inform design decisions presented with accessible graphs. The project progresses with UXPin prototype iterations in tandem with usability testing.

Requirements Workshop

The Business Model Canvas was a quick method of gathering key words for content and listing key features. The workshop concluded with organising the features into an MVP.

3 key findings:

1) The requirements workshop revealed many content requirements that served user needs.

2) The proccess illuminated other marketing opportunites to pursue such as targeting flyers to nearby offices.

3) The client was considering implementing a personalised booking system. Discussion revealed that maintaining the booking system would disrupt the restaurant's workflow.

Key action: Implement chosen features and content into the initial prototype.

Google Analytics Breakdown

Graphs communicating key insights created with Adobe InDesign, Adobe Illustrator and Tableau.

3 key findings:

1) Almost 60% of visitors are using mobile devices - taking a mobile first approach was an appropriate decision.

2) Most users exit on the homepage and the menu pages.

3) The homepage and menu pages also have the highest bounce rate.

Key actions: Evaluate the problem pages against Nielson's usability heuristics and Peter Morville's 7 factors influencing the user experience. Examine the user flows and evaluate how users perform tasks in usability testing.

The Impact of Usability Testing on the Wireframes

Greyscale Prototypes made with UXPin. 5 users answered open questions around their decision-making process for selecting restaurants, followed by performing key tasks. Specific focus was given to feedback and interaction with the homepage and menu pages. The comparison below depicts changes before and after usability testing. Only the homepage and menu pages are shown here for brevity.

3 key findings:

1) Watching users select from restaurants using their mobiles, in addition to open questioning, revealed appealing images of food and galleries were pivotal.

2) Users expected the location and other important information to reside at the bottom of the homepage.

3) Users were frustrated navigating up and down the site hierarchy to reach different menus.

Key action: Implement solutions with new prototype.

Moving forwards: The clients agreed the schema would be used in the final design currently in development. Further A/B testing, usability testing and analytics are required post production.
